DECISION PURSUANT TO PEREMPTORY WRIT OF MANDAMUS

This matter was heard before a Commission on Professional Competence (commission) of the

Placer County Office of Education on July 13, 14, 15 and 17, 2009, in Auburn, California. The
commission issued a decision dismissing the accusation against respondent. The Placer County
Superintendent of Schools and the Placer County Office of Education filed a petition for peremptory
writ of mandamus, which was heard on May 4, 2010, in the Superior Court of the State of California,
County of Placer. On May 20, 2010, the Court entered its judgment granting the petition. A peremptory
writ of mandamus was issued ordering the commission to set aside its decision, and to enter a decision
stating cause for dismissal is established under Education Code section 44932, subdivisions (a)(1), (3),
(5) and (7). Accordingly, the following order is made:

ORDER

The decision of the Commission on Professional Competence is set aside. Cause for dismissal is

established under Education Code section 44932, subdivisions (a)(1), (3), (5) and (7).
